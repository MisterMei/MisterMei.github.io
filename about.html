My Running Story

How running transformed my life at 45

Hello, I'm a Runner

At 45, I've discovered that age is just a number when it comes to achieving your fitness goals. What started as a simple attempt to get healthier has evolved into a profound journey of self-discovery, discipline, and joy.

Based in Sydney, Australia, I've been fortunate to train on some of the world's most beautiful running routes. From the iconic coastal paths of Bondi to the serene loops of Centennial Park, every run is an opportunity to connect with nature and with myself.

This website documents my journey through marathons, training routes, and the lessons learned along the way. Whether you're a seasoned runner or just starting out, I hope my experiences inspire you to lace up your shoes and discover what you're capable of achieving.

My Running Journey

2017

The Beginning

Started running at 40 to improve health. Could barely run 2km without stopping. Set a goal to complete a 5km parkrun.

2018

First Marathon

Completed my first marathon in 5:08:19. It was brutal, emotional, and absolutely life-changing. I was hooked.

2019

Building Consistency

Ran two more marathons, improving each time. Learned the importance of proper training plans, nutrition, and recovery.

2020

Pandemic Running

Running became my sanctuary during lockdowns. Discovered the mental health benefits and the power of routine and discipline.

2021-2022

Breaking Barriers

Broke the 4-hour marathon barrier! Refined my training approach, focusing on consistency over intensity.

2023

Personal Bests

Set PBs in both half marathon (1:38:42) and continued improving full marathon times. Running stronger than ever at 45.

2024

New Heights

Achieved marathon PB of 3:45:23 at Sydney Marathon. Sharing my journey to inspire other mid-life runners.

My Training Approach

Weekly Structure

  • Monday: Easy recovery run (5-8km)
  • Tuesday: Speed work or intervals
  • Wednesday: Rest or cross-training (swimming/yoga)
  • Thursday: Tempo run (8-12km)
  • Friday: Easy run (5-8km)
  • Saturday: Long run (15-30km depending on cycle)
  • Sunday: Rest or very easy recovery jog

Key Principles

  • 80/20 rule: 80% easy pace, 20% hard efforts
  • Progressive overload: gradually increase distance/intensity
  • Recovery weeks: every 3-4 weeks, reduce volume by 30%
  • Nutrition: whole foods, adequate protein, hydration
  • Sleep: prioritize 7-8 hours for recovery
  • Strength training: 2x weekly core and leg work
